Project Overview

Counterscope is the public-facing interface of a Digital Humanities thesis submitted to the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at IIT Guwahati. The project engineers a four-tiered computational pipeline — automated archival, Regex-driven epistemological repair, object-oriented validation, and relational database construction — to liberate three monsoon seasons (2023, 2024, 2025) of ASDMA flood data from the PDF-enforced bureaucratic opacity of the FRIMS portal.

Theoretical Framework

The project draws on James C. Scott’s concept of “bureaucratic legibility” and Johanna Drucker’s distinction between data (given) and capta (taken), treating the state’s 25-metric taxonomy as an instrument of administrative seeing rather than a neutral record. The dashboard design resists Donna Haraway’s “God Trick” — the illusion of an omniscient, overhead view — in favour of a minimalist, query-driven interface.

Parsing Pipeline & Database Errors

The SQLite database produced by the pipeline contains three verifiable parsing failures. This dashboard explicitly documents these errors rather than silently correcting them — treating the failures themselves as evidence of bureaucratic formatting friction.

Error 1 — Human Deaths (all years)

0 across all 346 records. Death figures appear in a narrative prose row the Regex extractor missed. Corrected: 65 (2023), 52 (2024), ≥27 (2025).

Error 2 — Districts Affected

12 Jul 2023: reads 2023 (year misread as district count). All 2024 & 2025 rows: read 0 — PDF layout changed; parser did not adapt.

Error 3 — Embankment Breaches
